Life Insurance Product Comparison

Whole Life Insurance

Permanent coverage with cash value. Guaranteed death benefit + savings component.
  • Lifetime coverage
  • Fixed premiums
  • Cash value grows tax-deferred
  • Guaranteed payout
  • Higher premiums than term

Cons: High cost, slow cash growth, complexity, loan interest reduces benefit.

Term Life Insurance

Temporary coverage (10/20/30 years). Pure protection, no cash value.
  • Lower premiums
  • High coverage ($50K‚Äì$1M+)
  • Medical exam often required
  • Renewable/convertible

Cons: Expires, no payout if outlived, premiums rise with age.

Universal Life Insurance

Permanent + flexible premiums & benefits. Cash value tied to interest/investments.
  • Lifelong coverage
  • Adjustable premiums & death benefit
  • Cash value growth (variable)
  • Tax advantages

Cons: Complex, higher fees, lapse risk, fluctuating growth.

Why Reviewing Older Policies Matters

Inflation's Impact:
A $20,000 policy in 2005 is equivalent to $30,250 in 2025.
A $20,000 policy in 2015 is equivalent to $24,000 in 2025.
This means coverage from 10-20 years ago has less purchasing power today.

Objection Handling

What is an Objection?
An objection in a sales call refers to a client or prospect expressing a concern, hesitation, or disagreement regarding a product, service, or offer. Objections can arise due to various reasons, such as cost, perceived value, fit with their needs, trust, competition, or any other factor that creates resistance to making a purchasing decision.

Reasons Clients May Drop the Call

  • Lack of Interest: The client may not currently see a need for life insurance or may not be interested in discussing financial planning at that moment.
  • Previous Negative Experience: The client may have had a negative experience with life insurance in the past or may have heard negative stories from others, leading them to be skeptical or uninterested.
  • Financial Constraints: The client may have budgetary constraints or may perceive life insurance as an additional financial burden that they cannot afford at the moment.
  • Time Constraints: The client may be busy or have limited time to engage in a detailed discussion about life insurance during the phone call.
  • Misalignment with Needs: The client may have already purchased life insurance from another provider or may not see the offered policy aligning with their specific needs.
  • Preference for Other Financial Products: The client may have a preference for other financial products or investments and may not prioritize life insurance at that time.

Tips for Effective Objection Handling

  • Ensure you have great tonality: Sounding genuine and concerned will most likely have a calming and positive effect on your client.
  • Be an active listener. To build trust with your prospect, it's essential that they feel like you're actually hearing their concerns.
  • Use empathy to validate the prospect's concerns. A great technique to handle all types of objections is to empathize with your prospect and let them know that you understand where they're coming from. Once you do that, the prospect will lower their guard, and they'll be more open to accept a solution.
  • Build Value: To keep your client engaged and interested, they must see value in our product.
  • Please note that excitement is key in every call. The more excited you are, the less, the chances of your client saying they can‚Äôt talk considering they already picked up.
  • Additionally when speaking to clients do not ask do you have time to speak this will help reduce objections all together.
